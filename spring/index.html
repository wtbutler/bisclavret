The storm crashed around him, wind blowing across the mouth of the cave and rain pelting the mouth almost rhythmically. He hated the rain. It always made him colder than snow did. It bled into his fur, the cold sinking into his bones. He barely remembered what it was like to be in a house during a storm. To be warm, dry, protected. To sit by the fireplace as the crackling hearth drowned out the distant thunder. Maybe he remembered more than he thought he had. He wished he didn't. It would make not having it so much easier. He waited, a sodden tangle of fur, until the storm blew itself out and died down to a faint wind. The clouds scattered, and the moon came out, its squashed shape still bright enough to light the night.
