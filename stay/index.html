The howls grew closer, separating into individual barks and bays. The sound of hounds was a frightening thing when one was the prey, but he wasn't worried. There were plenty of other things that would be better to hunt. The sounds drew even nearer. Perhaps it was best to start moving away, just in case. He started to trot away, at a pace that he could maintain for miles. The howls followed him. Uncertainly bubbled in him, but determination shoved it down, as he broke into a faster paced run. This couldn't be happening. He couldn't be prey, it wasn't supposed to work like this. When the howls didn't fade into the distance, he turned towards the nearest river, running at a dead sprint. He had to lose them, somehow. He splashed into the river and waded in the banks upstream for a half a mile, all the while the howls followed him. He finally tracked back across onto the same side, hoping they'd assume he'd swum across.
