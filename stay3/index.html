He shot through trees, in between gaps, and sometimes under roots. He knew this area well, so he could navigate it quickly, but he could feel it wouldn't be enough. The dogs were clumsy louts, tripping over themselves and uncertain of how to follow quickly, but there were so many of them that it hardly mattered. He couldn't do anything to hide himself any more. He could feel himself tiring, and there was only a mile or so before the tangle opened up again. He simply couldn't keep this pace up any more. He slowed, and the hounds sensed his exhaustion, two breaking free on his right. They ran at him, forcing him to turn. They slowed a bit. They weren't trying to hurt him just yet, just moving him. He could see the bloodlust in their eyes, though. They would attack if he refused to cooperate.
